# Should I take a loan to launch faster?

An idea validated against this brain, in the standard format.

## Supports

- Acting now beats waiting for perfect conditions: efficient action in the
  present compounds ([[acting-in-the-present]]).
- If the loan funds building genuinely new value rather than fighting for a
  fixed market, it fits the creative frame ([[creative-vs-competitive-mind]]).

## Contradicts or qualifies

- Barnum's strongest warning is against debt: it makes you a servant of the
  creditor, and consumer or speculative debt is a ruin risk
  ([[economy-and-avoiding-debt]]). This directly cautions against borrowing to
  launch.
- Both sources favor [[perseverance]] with what you have over leverage as a
  shortcut.

## Not covered

- The brain has no coverage on the modern distinction between productive leverage
  (financing a clearly cash-flowing asset) and consumer debt, nor on interest
  rates, runway, or unit economics. These are decisive here and sit outside these
  1880-1910 texts.

## Verdict

The brain leans **against** debt-funded speed: use Barnum's floor (avoid debt,
keep the earn-save gap) and Wattles' bias to act now with the resources you
already have. Because the sources conflict on leverage and omit modern finance,
confidence is **medium**; validate the numbers (runway, unit economics) outside
this brain before deciding.
