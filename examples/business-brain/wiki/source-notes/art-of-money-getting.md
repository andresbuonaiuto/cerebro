# The Art of Money Getting (P. T. Barnum, 1880)

## Central thesis

Making and keeping money is a practical art built on plain rules: live within
your means, pick the right vocation and location, stay healthy, persevere, and
avoid the traps (debt, speculation, over-diversification) that ruin most people.

## Main arguments

- **Economy first.** Real economy is spending less than you earn, on the right
  things. Small habitual leaks sink fortunes. See [[economy-and-avoiding-debt]].
- **Avoid debt.** Debt makes a man a slave to his creditor; young people should
  dread it. See [[economy-and-avoiding-debt]].
- **The right vocation and location.** Match work to temperament, and set up
  where that work can thrive.
- **Perseverance.** Persistence through early failure separates those who
  succeed from those who quit. See [[perseverance]].
- **Focus over scattering.** "Do not scatter your powers": succeed in one line
  before branching out.

## Citable points

- Barnum frames economy not as stinginess but as proportion: many who earn well
  stay poor because "it is not what a man earns, but what he saves" (economy
  chapter, paraphrase).
- The rule against debt and the rule against scattering effort are the most
  actionable for a modern operator.

## Limitations

- A lecture, not a system: rules are anecdotal and drawn from 19th-century
  American commerce. Specifics (advertising, trades) are dated even where the
  principles are not.
