# Mindset vs method

The two sources approach wealth from opposite ends, and reading them together is
more useful than either alone.

- **Wattles ([[science-of-getting-rich]])** works top-down from mindset: adopt a
  [[creative-vs-competitive-mind]], hold a grateful vision, and take efficient
  action ([[acting-in-the-present]]). The inner state drives the outcome.
- **Barnum ([[art-of-money-getting]])** works bottom-up from behavior: control
  spending ([[economy-and-avoiding-debt]]), pick the right vocation, focus, and
  persist. The habits drive the outcome.

Where they agree, confidence is highest: both insist on [[perseverance]]. A
practical reading is to use Barnum's rules as the operating floor (do not go into
consumer debt, keep the earn-save gap, focus) and Wattles' creative framing as
the direction (build new value rather than fight for a fixed share).

## Contradiction

The sources disagree on **competition**.

- Wattles: get rich with a *creative* mind and leave competition behind entirely;
  competing for a fixed pool is the wrong frame ([[creative-vs-competitive-mind]]).
- Barnum: assumes an ordinary *competitive* commercial world and coaches you to
  win in it through economy, focus, and perseverance.

This brain does not resolve the conflict. Treat "creative vs competitive" as a
useful bias toward value creation, not as a claim that competition is unreal.
Confidence on this synthesis is **low** precisely because the sources conflict.
