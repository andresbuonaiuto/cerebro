# Perseverance

The one principle both sources share, which is why its confidence is **high**.

- **Barnum**: persistence through early failure is what separates those who
  succeed from those who quit; most give up just before the turn
  ([[art-of-money-getting]], perseverance chapter).
- **Wattles**: each act done in the "Certain Way" is a success that makes the
  next more likely, so consistent efficient action compounds over time
  ([[science-of-getting-rich]]).

Why it matters for decisions:

- When results lag, the two sources agree the default should be to continue
  refining and repeating the action, not to abandon it.
- Distinguish perseverance (repeating a sound action) from stubbornness
  (repeating a disproven one). Both authors assume the underlying approach is
  sound (inference, not stated in the sources).
