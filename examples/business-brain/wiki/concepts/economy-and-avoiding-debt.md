# Economy and avoiding debt

Barnum's financial core. **Economy** is spending less than you earn on the right
things, a matter of proportion rather than deprivation. **Debt** is its enemy: it
turns future earnings into an obligation and, in Barnum's framing, the debtor
into a servant of the creditor.

Why it matters for decisions:

- Protect the gap between earning and spending before chasing more revenue: "it
  is not what a man earns, but what he saves" ([[art-of-money-getting]],
  economy chapter, paraphrase).
- Treat consumer debt and speculative leverage as ruin risks, not tools.

Caveats:

- Single source. Barnum predates modern credit; productive leverage (financing a
  clearly profitable asset) is a real exception his blanket rule does not address
  (inference, not stated in the source).
