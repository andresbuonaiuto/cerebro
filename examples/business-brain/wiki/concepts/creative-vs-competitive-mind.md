# Creative vs competitive mind

Wattles' central distinction. The **competitive mind** sees wealth as a fixed
pool and tries to take a larger share from others. The **creative mind** brings
new value into being, so getting rich does not require anyone else to lose.

Why it matters for decisions:

- A creative framing pushes you toward making a bigger market rather than
  fighting for a slice of an existing one.
- It reframes competitors as evidence of demand, not as enemies to be beaten.

Caveats:

- Asserted as principle, not proven (single source, [[science-of-getting-rich]]).
- In practice most businesses face real competitive constraints; the idea is a
  useful bias, not a denial of competition (inference, not stated in the source).
