# Acting in the present

Wattles' answer to paralysis. You cannot act where you are not, or at a time that
is not now. So the path to a bigger future is to do today's work efficiently,
"more than filling" your present place, rather than waiting for better
conditions.

Why it matters for decisions:

- Bias toward the next concrete action you can take today over planning for
  conditions that do not yet exist.
- "Efficient action" compounds: each well-done act makes the next more likely to
  succeed, which links to [[perseverance]].

Caveats:

- Single source ([[science-of-getting-rich]]). The idea complements, but does not
  replace, deliberate planning.
